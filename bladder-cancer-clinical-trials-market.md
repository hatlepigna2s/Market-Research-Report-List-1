<p><p>Bladder cancer clinical trials often utilize various imaging technologies to assess disease progression and treatment efficacy. CT scans provide detailed cross-sectional images, helping to identify tumors and metastases. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) offers high-resolution images of soft tissues, aiding in staging and locational assessment. Bone scans detect bone metastases through radioactive tracers, while chest X-rays are used to identify lung involvement or metastasis. Together, these imaging modalities are crucial in monitoring bladder cancer and evaluating clinical trial outcomes.</p></p>

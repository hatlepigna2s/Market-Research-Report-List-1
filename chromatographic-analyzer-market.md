<p><p>Chromatographic analyzers are categorized into three primary market types based on the phase utilized in separation processes. Gas-phase chromatography employs gases as the mobile phase, ideal for volatile substances. Liquid-phase chromatography, on the other hand, uses liquids, making it suitable for a wider range of compounds, including those that are non-volatile or thermally unstable. The "Other" category encompasses specialized techniques, including supercritical fluid chromatography and ion chromatography, which cater to specific analytical needs and applications across various industries.</p></p>

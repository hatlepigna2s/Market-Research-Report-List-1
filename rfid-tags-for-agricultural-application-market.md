<p><p>RFID tags are essential in agriculture for tracking and managing livestock, crops, and equipment. Active RFID tags have their own power source, enabling longer read ranges and enabling real-time data collection but are generally more expensive. Passive RFID tags, on the other hand, rely on an external reader for power, making them more cost-effective and suitable for large-scale applications, though they have shorter read ranges. Both types enhance efficiency, improve inventory management, and promote traceability in agricultural practices.</p></p>

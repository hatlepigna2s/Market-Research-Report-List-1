<p><p>The Anti-Tuberculosis Drug market is primarily divided into two categories: First-Line and Second-Line Anti-Tuberculosis Drugs. First-Line drugs, such as isoniazid and rifampicin, are the primary treatment for drug-susceptible tuberculosis due to their effectiveness and lower side effects. In contrast, Second-Line drugs are utilized for multi-drug resistant tuberculosis cases and include medications like fluoroquinolones and injectable agents. These categories emphasize the varying treatment strategies based on the type and resistance of the tuberculosis strain, impacting both market dynamics and therapeutic approaches.</p></p>
